After 8 years of using ever decreasing in size non apple laptops I've now switched from a tiny, 10 inch Windows laptop to a massive 16inch slab of Apple silicon. Getting back into the Mac eco system was pretty smooth - a week or two of retraining the muscle memory and then back to working as smoothly as every. In fact even smoother - for all my success with non Mac's it's been quite a joy to come back - everything just works - the camera is good, the microphone is good, it looks good and above all - the battery life is amazing. It's a huge slab of a laptop compared to my teeny laptop but doesn't need all the extras I needed to lug around to last a full day.

Tiny tiny windows laptops

I've previously written about my teeny tiny powerful computer. A tiny 10 inch laptop that was super portable. However there was no camera, the batter life was poor, the screen and keyboard were small. Most days I'd pack up a spare screen, keyboard, battery pack, charger. Hey - it was super portable if needed it to be - but most of the time I didn't need the it's teeny tininnes. In my mind we'd be hiking across hills and pausing to update a PR, running down a remote beach and stopping to add a blog post, swimming across a lake and then drying off to fix a bug. In reality I was based at home or a short trip from a v well equiped co working space.

Return of the Mac

I didn't go full in on the 16incher - that would of been too much - I eased in slowly with a delightful 14 inch Mac. I joined a growing startup in April 2025 - and they gave everyone a super souped up apple laptop. This was my first shift from 8 years of Linux, Windows and WSL. But in that time I've kept updating my working setup - learning new shortcuts, trying new software so another update wasn't a big shock. Switching to a Mac was pretty smooth - and to be fair, in almost every situation the Mac hardware or software just worked slightly smoother, better, more preofessionsally.

Size

A second job in 2025 herralded a new laptop. A smaller tech company so I inherited the laptop of the person I was taking over from. This was the biggest laptop I've ever used professionally. Like getting a new TV it felt huge to start - but after a short period you get used to it and anything else smaller becomes almost unusable. I'm not hiking great distances - three times a week I walk the 20mins to london fields. The bigger laptop means no extra screen, no extra camera. I do take an extra keyboard as I put the laptop on a stand for ergonomic reasons. And the best thinf of all has been the amazing battery life. A day of calls I still need a charger but for most days I can get by with just the overnight charge

Summary

The shift to mac has been great - learnt a huge amount from being on windows and linux for 8 years but it's just happy making being back with a Mac. Maybe i've missed out on the battery improvement burst from the new windows ai PCs but overall the Mac package just works and it's pretty smooth. Hey - I know how my prod software is getting insalled on linux but I don't need to worry about that day to day.

Size? well - the 16 inch took a little while to get used to. I think the 14 inch would be my ideal. My setup and workflow means I'm happy without too much screen real estate so I think the 14inch size would be fine. I do have an eye on a even smaller 8inch PC and a VR headset but that's a post for another day. For now - the Mac is back - and it's big!
