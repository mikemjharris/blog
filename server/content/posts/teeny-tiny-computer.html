The last few years i've been on a side quest to find a super tiny but powerful computer. I've tried remote computing with a chromebook, pushed a surface go to it's limit and was resigned to sticking with a 13 incher for day to day work. But then I found in the depths of Ali express the One-Netbook 5 - Intel I7, 32gb RAM and 10 inches - but was it any good?

Replacing a Dell XPS 13

My last few computers have been Dell XPS 13s. The new teeny tiny computer was meant to be either a temporary replacement for my last one that didn't have enough power. I'd hope for greawt things but assumed a small computer wouldn't be up to being my main laptop. But it was! Nine months later and that teeny tiny computer is my main day to day workhorse.

Not only did I have the speed but I had amazing portability - see the pictures for how easily the laptop fits on a train and in comparison with a 13inch Mac.

Downsides

Compared to the Dell - not really any. But both the Dell and the teeny tiny computer both have poor (or indeed no!) camera and relatively poor battery life. I got a super portable Opal tadpole camera and a battery pack (see pics). Both of these I would use anyway with another windows laptop.

Size

The size is amazing - easy to use just about anywhere and powerful enough to do all my day to day work tasks. The keyboard is at the limit of what's usable and I needed to relearn a few keystrokes but for me the benefit of such a small and light laptop outweighs that. Most of the time though it's plugged in at home to a big screen with a seperate keyboard. It's size isn't that relevant then but when I'm out and about it slots into a small shoulder bag and I'm off.

Summary

If you like small laptops like I do then it's brilliant. The downsides of the camera and battery a minor inconvenience and am hoping a future version with the new Copilot pcs will fix those issues. For now I love that it slots into a small shoulder bag and I'm off!
