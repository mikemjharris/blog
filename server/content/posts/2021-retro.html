Well crickey - what a year! In last year's retro I reckoned we'd have more of the same pandemic life but I think we all hoped for the vaccine saviour narrative. As that seemed to unfold and life opened up the breath we'd all been holding for 2020 started to be released - sometimes in smooth releases, sometimes in violent, hard sobs. Lots of the stress and tension seemed to come out this year - lots of life opening up but, certainly in my frend circle, it seemed like we had to deal with a year and a half of life in a short time. Fun and maybe too exciting. Life but trauma playing out. On top of it all a weariness and lack of energy. But how does all that chaos reflect in the numbers?

Stats - running for the year

Stats wise? Numbers wise kinda like 2017/16 but with a few more books read. And what's that last one? Cycling? Never tracked that properly - what's that all about? The number that most reflects my year is the KM run. We started off with monotonous lockdown - the running was steady. I around half the total for the year in the first three months. But I struggled with a lack of yoga. I went on an excessivley long run to prove I'm still young on my 40th birthday. Obviously that half injured me but I'd signed up for an ultra. It was the first thing open post lockdown and stuborness meant I ran round most of it with a friend and limped the final 10k. And that probably is the best metaphor for the year - desperate to get out and do everything - but overdoing it. Loving it all, but taking on too much and being underprepared. Still I made it to the end of the run and I made it to the end of the year - with what might be a a grimace, or even perhaps a smile.

Cycling

So cycling was probably the big revelation this year. Early in the year I wrote about pedalling through a pandemic about the online zwift league I'd raced in over the winter. As hoped this translated to real world cycling - lots of rides with the neighbour opposite, other freinds and colleagues. A highlight was 100km tour of the peak district - again early days of life opening up and this went much better than my runnign ultra! Amazing views and a novelty to have a trip away with friends - salve for the body and soul.

On top of normal cycling I also had a go at bike packing - a number of twitter friends/aquaintances/randoms who I follow seem to have done something similar. As with many people I had a craving to do something different and also get out into nature. Strapping a bunch of bags to you and pedalling off into the distance was certainly an excellent way to spend time. Slowly got into it and hoping that the investigative work this year will mean some longer trips in the futures.

New years resolutions

Given the uncertaintity last year the only resolution I half mentioned was doing more creative writing. I did a bit - joing a few of the excellent write and shine sessions. Much of the year was laying down the foundations - following the right people, buying the books - eyeing up a new hobby working out how to fit it into life. More of this to come over the next few years I hope.

With uncertaintity last year setting big goals was foolish - but in the back of my mind I was turning 40. As a middle class, middel aged, white man one of the recognised ways to celebrate a big milestone is to do an excssive sporting event. Certainly that's how I celebrated my 30th (content warning - me in a dress). At the end of last year I signed up for my first half ironman and hoped that would go ahead and I'd be in shape. Covid played ball and despite my running injuries and a DNF at the London triathlon I made it round. Tick that midlife cisis box.

Swimming and yoga

Not much to say here - swimming - some cold water/open water at the start of the year - but as pools opened stuck with them. Holidays happend and a much needed trip abroad with the excellent swim trek folks. Yoga too got a boost - studios opened and being in the warm snug embrace of the hot pod yoga studio was a massive boost.

Olivia Laings - Funny Weather - Art in an Emergency, Ted Chiang's Exhalation, Anuk Arudpragasam's A Passage North. Art? Paula Rego's exhibition at the Tate a clear winner. David Hockney's Arrival of Spring though was a perfect tonic for the year - simple easy but joyful. Loads more and some plays, shows, life, culture. Was it good? Or just good to be out?

Life

So much more happening in the world - it felt like lots of people going througha tough time later on in the year. For vairous reasons I ended up living with friend who I shared a flat with 20 years ago. Back then there was a fairly momentous few months - my friend had cancer treatment and didn't want to leave London so I nursed him through that. Sharing a flat againg 20 years on we discussed that we both had little recollection of what actually happened - with the optimisim and joyful ignorance of youth we just sailed through it and then blocked it out. In a matter of history repeating itself he got covid and I delivered food to his door for ten days.

Not ideal, but cathartic for both of us. Hey - there's not always an upside when things go wrong but sometimes there is. Lots of friends going through bumpy patches - some wont work out - at least for now. Some will. I think the hardest thing is everyone is runnign out of puff. I certainly feel guilty about not being as supportive or available as I feel I would of been a few years ago. But everyone is the same and when you finally catch up everyone apologises for being a bit rubbish. One wish for next year for everyone is some downtime and recovery.

Mentoring

Weirdly this time last year I ended up starting mentoring four different people. Three from friends who managed the person and suggested I might help - one from a Generaly Assembly talk who approached me after. The pandemic meant there was plenty of time and we'd all got used to hoping on a call. As life opened up two of the mentees dropped off. The other two continue to be part of my monthly routine. I've learnt a huge amount in the process - I love chatting to people, learning how they see the world and how they are navigating their careers. I believe (and have been told) I've listened, offered a space to discuss issues and given my thoughts and stories when needed. I've really enjoyed mentoring - it's very different from being a manager (although with many overlapping skills) - being distant but close is a unique position and am really happy that I think I have helped my mentees out and also grown and learnt myself. The best thing to happen this year.

Conclusion

Well another year! Ups and downs, highs and lows - runnign fast then dealing with injury and over excitement. Who knows what the new year brings. For me the usual mix of hobbies with even more creative writing. Hopefully some more bikepacking, perhaps some travel - but overall just taking it a bit more steady this year. No need to go hurtling along too quickly - a bit steadier, a bit slower, a bit more thougtful and life should all be good.
