Running more doesn't necessarily mean a good year of running

Last year I ran the most I'd ever run. This year I ran 50% more than last year! The main reason was signing up to my first ever ultra with a couple of friends. We ran round the Isle of Wight (106km) one Saturday in May. Lots of ways to train for long races but I went for the option of running more but at lower intensity. Fitted this into my week mainly by doing a few more commute runs and slightly longer weekend runs. Also while taking some time off after big runs I made sure I got back into at least low level training quicker than previous years. Am getting older and find having an extended fallow period makes getting back into training pretty tough.

Loved the ultra and have really loved the trail running community. Post IOW ran two more ultras (50k in Tring in summer and 50k in Surrey hills in Dec). In addition ran the London marathon as part of my training and did Hackney half with techrunners (and suprised myself with my fastest time on that course despite lack of speed training). Techrunners I stepped back in the summer - being the main person responsible for organising the weekly runs is quite tough over a long time and I needed a breather.

Next year I'm moving jobs and will have a super short commute. Whilst I can definitely make the run home longer it's hard to push and do those extra KM on days when you are tired. I expect next year to run less but am planning on doing more high quality runs - hoping to join some track and hill training sessions. Main aim of the year is to run a London marathon qualifying time in Milton Keynes marathon in may and then run a couple of ultra trail runs.

Swimmign & Yoga

This year swam the 1 mile Looe island swim - it's where my Grandma lives and meant a lot to do a big swim in the sea where we played around as kids. Next year planning on the same one and looking at some longer distance ones too.

After experimenting with self pracitce a previous year I'm back in the swing of classes - mainly at Hot Pod Yoga in hackney. Have made sure I had time to do the yoga to balance the extra running I do - generally when I run but don't do yoga I get injured - this year has been fine.

January I have a month off and am going to be completing my 200 hour yoga teacher training in Bali. Super excited as has been a goal of mine to do for a while now and luckily managed to organise time off between jobs to finally get to do it. Expect next years yoga numbers to be through the roof!

The art world

Similarly to last year this year has been hugely impacted by working at the RA. Knowing I probably wouldn't be in the traditional art world forever I've made the most of the opportuities visiting a huge amount of galleries and exhibitions. The benefit of free entry (which an RA staff pass gives you) is that I don't feel obliged to stay in an exhibiton I don't enjoy and also means I try out ones I would not otherwise of done. For example popped into Van Gogh and Britain in Tate britain - didn't engage with it much so was done quickly - with the extra time decided to pop into Frank Bowling's exhibiton which was maginificient and delightful and one of my top exhibitons for the year. That would be rounded out by the RA's Gormley exhbiiton and Lee Herring at Tate Liverpool.

The arts community has been fantastic - art twitter is so welcomign and enjoyed joining in with Museum Selfie day and sharing Museum Bums pics. It's also been fantastic being involved with the Tessitura project at the RA - this is a system that manges tickets, membership, crm and more for arts organisations. Through that community I've met so many people and enjoyed lots of other experiences - specifically got invited to see a number of plays. My favourite by a long way (and maybe the best play I've ever seen?!) was Fairview at the Young Vic.

Two years in the arts has been brilliant - it's a symbiotic relationship - I've developed professionally (learnt lots about managing and tech) but also personally - both in being imersed in art and the confidence it's given me to learn more, read more and experience more things. On the flipside I've helped deliver a a big project for the RA on time and within budget and really enjoyed helping my team progress and develop.

Fortunately my new job is still creatively based - I'll be working at Wonderbly which created personalised childrens books. There are lots of writers and artists working alongside the tech team and excited to see what new things I learn from them. For the next year I imagine I'll see fewer exhibitons but going to try and keep a goo balance of creative, arty pursuits up. It's a good balance combined with the sport and tech.
