Preparing for a talk

I've given a number of talks over the years in a wide range of circumstances. This was the biggest tech talk I've given and the first where I've been representing a company. This talk was about MOO and what we as a team have achieved and developed. The way I prepared the speach is similar to how I have prepared for many other speaches:

  • Once I find out I am talking take some time for it to sit at the back of my mind and sink in
  • Start preparing the talk verbally on my commutes in. I ride my bike and spend the commute talking.
  • After a couple of weeks talking to myself try and build out a very basic slide deck - this makes you realise that some of the things that work in you mind while cycling through Hackney don't make sense in a real presentation.
  • Keep talking to myself on my commutes - every few days go back over the slides
  • Closer to the event practice as much as possible with a computer and standing up (not on a bike) as if you were at a conference
  • Practise...
  • ...even more
  • On the day of the conference have as much under your control as possible.
  • Take a deep breath, smile and enjoy it...!

What went well and where could I have improved

In anything I do I try to celebrate and enjoy the parts that went well and try and learn from areas I feel I could improve. Most things in life are never or as bad as you think.

Things that went wellI had practised lots, the speech went well, I was confident and knew my stuff. I enjoyed myself and was happy with my performance.

Things I could of done betterI generally talk fast and try and slow down as much as possible when presenting - I did a good job but could of gone slower. There wasn't anything humourous of funny in the talk - this was a big corportate conference and I was representing MOO rather than myself and I errred on the side of professionalism. I think that I could of introduced some more light hearted sections.

Overall the talk was well received - I got some good feedback and had some great questions/discussions post talk. The overall conference was fantastic - I learnt a huge amount over two days. The first day was mainly big talks - the second day was more of the same with the option of some small 'un-conference' break out talks which which worked very well. Will hopefully be back next year!
